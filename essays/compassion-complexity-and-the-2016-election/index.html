Compassion, Complexity, and the 2016 U.S. Election

A look into America's approach at modern day politics through the lens of social media.

The United States election is exactly two days from the time I am writing this post. It seems like it's been a year that the only news related to the presidential candidates matters. In the timespan though things have happened all over the world from the 70 year reign of the king of Thailand coming to an end, to Rome electing its first female mayor, and who can forget Britain's decision to leave the European Union. The two main party candidates will not be discussed in this discourse as there's been enough talk of them for a lifetime. What this will explore is the constant dismissal and belittling of others opinions that has been rampant in this election and why that is a poignantly and frighteningly un-American aspect of this election.

Spend any amount of time on the internet and you will see the constant barrage of people calling others who support their opposing party ignorant, evil, and anti-american. This occurs in every election, because many people have the tendency to believe that they are standing on the correct side of an issue or a decision. If people thought they were wrong then they would flip to the other side of the issue and think they are correct.
